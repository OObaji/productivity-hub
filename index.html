EMS Tag Information

Tag Types

Radio Frequency (RF)

Used strictly for curfew monitoring. Communicates with a Home Monitoring Unit (HMU). Triggers an absence if the tag leaves the HMU's range during curfew hours.

Global Positioning System (GPS)

Provides 24/7 location tracking. Used to enforce exclusion zones (places the subject cannot go) or inclusion zones (places they must be). Requires daily charging.

Alcohol Monitoring (AAMR)

Transdermal tag that tests sweat for alcohol concentration. Usually takes readings every 30 minutes to enforce abstinence requirements.

Non-Fitted Devices (PID)

Portable Identification Devices or biometric checkers used in specific scenarios where a fitted tag isn't suitable or is used as a supplementary tracker.

Common Violations & Breaches

✂️
Strap Tamper The fibre-optic or conductive wire inside the strap has been severed or stretched, indicating an attempt to remove the tag.
🔨
Case Tamper The main body of the tag or the HMU has been opened, damaged, or subjected to extreme force/temperature.
🏃
Absence The DW (Device Wearer) is out of range of the HMU during their specified curfew hours.
🔋
Battery Depletion The DW failed to charge their GPS or AAMR tag, resulting in a loss of monitoring data.
🗺️
Zone Breach A GPS wearer has entered an exclusion zone or left a mandated inclusion zone.

Terminology

DW Device Wearer (the subject).
HMU Home Monitoring Unit.
PID Portable Identification Device.
NFA No Further Action.
AAMR Alcohol Abstinence Monitoring Requirement.
Decom Decommissioning (removing equipment).

Standard Protocols

Power Issues If GPS is below 10%, DW must be contacted to charge the tag immediately. It requires 1-2 hours daily.
Medical Holds If DW is in hospital, verify location and log "NFA - DW in Hospital". Tag may need to be removed for MRI/X-rays.
Equipment Fault If the charger is broken or strap is causing medical issues (swelling, allergic reaction), log a Visit Request for a field officer.
